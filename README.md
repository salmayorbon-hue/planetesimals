    <h2>Etymology</h2>
    <p>
         TThe word planetesimals comes from Greek and Latin roots:

planet- (Greek planētēs) meaning wanderer (used for celestial bodies that move in the sky)

esimal (from Latin -esimus) meaning small part or fraction


Literally, it means “small wandering celestial bodies” or small objects that can form planets.

    </p>

    <h2>Definition</h2>
    <p>
        Planetesimals are small celestial objects composed of rock, metal, or ice that orbit a star and can collide and combine to form planets or other larger bodies in a solar system.
    </p>

    <h3>Denotation</h3>
    <p>
        The denotation is the literal, objective meaning:
    <ul>
        <li>A Tiny astronomical objects in space that are the building blocks of planets.
</li>
        <li</li>
    </ul>
